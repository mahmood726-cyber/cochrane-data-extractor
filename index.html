Can a pipeline turn Cochrane's open data into research-ready meta-analytic datasets at scale?

An automated Python pipeline that harvests Cochrane systematic reviews into standardised, ready-to-pool effect-size tables — the open Pairwise70 corpus of 501 meta-analyses spanning 82,379 individual studies.

The Project

The extractor walks the Cochrane Library, downloads each review's published data package, parses the archived CSV tables into one standardised effect-size record per study, and appends study year, author, and DOI — with resumable checkpointing so an interrupted run continues without loss or duplication. The result is a single open corpus that the meta-research community can pool, stress-test, and benchmark against.

Validation & Robustness

Does the extraction hold up?

Two independent checks: the extractor was validated against manual extraction on a pilot set, and the bundled pooling engine was validated against the reference metafor package across the corpus.

The Hartung-Knapp-Sidik-Jonkman check matters: re-running 850 analyses, 45 results that were "significant" under the standard random-effects model lose significance once the small-sample correction is applied (264 → 219). A corpus this size makes that fragility measurable rather than anecdotal.
